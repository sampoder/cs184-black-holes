Summary:  We’re building an interactive 3D black hole simulator that’ll be interactive. It’s going to be a continuation of what we learnt about ray-tracing but light doesn’t work the same way around black holes which will make this difficult.

Problem Description

Here you should provide the context for your idea. Describe the problem that you are trying to solve, why it is important, where it is challenging. Give us a general idea on how you are going to solve it.

Our idea is to build an interactive 3D black hole simulator. Most of our work in this class (including the ray tracing project) operated under the standard assumption that light rays travel in a straight line, so tracing is fairly straightforward. However, near black holes, light can curve/bend and no longer follows that assumption. Using standard rendering techniques thus no longer accurately shows what these areas look like. We are trying to solve this problem and show effects such as gravitational lensing and redshift by using a new set of tracing principles. We will review the physics behind it (at the level needed for rendering) and read the papers behind geodesic ray marching to get an intuitive understanding for the problem. The solution uses the result of integrating the geodesic equations in spacetime to compute each pixel (light ray) value. There are separate cases that need to be handled for each location (eg. treat rays near the horizon) differently and there is also the opportunity to model the gravitational effects of the hole on nearby ‘masses’ such as dust clouds, creating unique graphical effects.

Goals and Deliverables

We are aiming to create an interactive black hole visualization that combines real-time rendering with a simplified gravitational simulation. Key goals of the project are to:

  • Real-time black hole simulator
  • Black center and glowing disk of light surrounding
  • Interactive controls to
  • Change light colors / scene appearance
  • Generate moving object that will be thrown into orbit
  • Implement a simplified gravity model that causes objects or orbit the black hole with particles spiraling inward and becoming removed when they get close to the center (get sucked into the black hole)

Our final deliverables will include:

  • An interactive simulation where users can
  • Modify color and lighting visual parameters
  • Spawn objects into orbit
  • Observe the objects orbiting, decaying inward, and being absorbed
  • Recorded video demonstrating multiple scenarios
  • A collection of rendered scenes, highlighting key visual effects

We plan to deliver realistic-looking images of black holes. We plan for our images to be in 3D and for our camera to be movable around the black hole and through space. Lastly, we plan for a basic level of interactivity. This is where we can be as ambitious as we want. The most basic version of interactivity will be able to change the parameters of the lighting. Our ambition is to do more than that though, we’d like to add the ability for people to insert objects into the scene and have these objects interact with the blackhole. It would also be nice if there were ways to change the black hole’s properties; for example, the mass.
