Our goal with this project was to create a black hole simulator similar to the ray-tracing assignment in Homework 3. So far, we’ve got a basic black hole to render! Here you can see some examples that we’ve rendered:

Inside of our rendering, you’re able to move the camera by orbiting around the black hole as well as zooming in and out of the black hole.

At the moment, the renderer is quite slow. To make up for this we render low resolution versions of the black hole as you move / change the camera. Then, once the user has stopped moving the camera we render a higher resolution image. This allows the users to get a sense of the movement they made.

An important thing to improve going forward will be improving the speed of the renderer (potentially using some of the optimisation methods we learnt about and implemented in Homework 3). Additionally, after optimising the render, it would be good to implement some sort of time-based rendering where objects move over time (as they are affected by gravity). This would also allow us to add a feature where users can spawn objects into the scene and then see how they are affected by gravity over time.

It would also be interesting to explore allowing the user to add new light sources with different colours and of different sizes / intensities to see how that could affect the scene. For example, adding a really large star close to the black hole!

We’re a little behind on our plan as we’re about halfway through the Week 2 goals at the moment but we feel good about building on top of what we already have so far.
